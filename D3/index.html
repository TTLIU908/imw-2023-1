When thinking about the metaphor of a website, I think of it as a digital garden. Like a garden, a website requires work, care, and attention in order to flourish. Each page is like a flowerbed where different ideas, content, and experiences are planted and nurtured. Design elements such as layout and navigation guide visitors through the digital landscape. Just as a garden reflects the personality and interests of the gardener, a website reflects the unique perspective, passion, and expertise of its creator. Visitors can wander through this digital garden, explore its nooks and crannies, discover new insights, and even find inspiration. Similar to a garden, a website is dynamic and evolving, with content growing and changing over time. It is a space for exploration, growth, and connection, where visitors can immerse themselves in the beauty of ideas and information.
